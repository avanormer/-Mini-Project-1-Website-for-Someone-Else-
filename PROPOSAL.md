## Client Brief Interview

**Client:** Deniz Ketenci, Current Director of The Service Benefit

**Purpose:** This website will serve as The Service Benefit's online platform to promote its mission beyond social media. It will feature content for several Filipino NGOs that The Service Benefit creates mini-commercials for, and link to the created content for the organizations themselves, since this community organization produces promotional media and content.

**Audience:** The audience for this website includes students, volunteers, and individuals who support various social causes that different NGOs address. Each NGO that The Service Benefit highlights and creates content for also benefits, as the organizations gain more recognition, support, and donations when their stories are shared on website platforms.

**Key action:** Visitors are encouraged to learn about different organizations' impact and the causes they support. Additionally, the website promotes calls to action such as donating or volunteering when people learn about each NGO's mission and backstory.

**Pages/sections needed:**
- Home: Summary of The Service Benefit's purpose, goals, and what it hopes to achieve and evoke in local communities
- About: Backstory of The Service Benefit and answers to "why" questions
- Each NGO's Project: Showcasing different Filipino NGOs that The Service Benefit has covered and what volunteer projects look like with each organization
- Impact Seen through Media Content: Embed videos and documentation of service events and outreach
- Call to Action: Information on how website visitors can connect with organizations to donate or volunteer

**Content status:**
Content Available: YouTube videos/shorts, Instagram Reels, photos, and videos collected from each service trip and work with each NGO
Content Needed: Personal message from the director, contact information for each NGO, and details on who to reach out to and how

**Style preferences:** Deniz wants a website that is simple yet demands action; she wants each NGO positively highlighted through images and inspirational descriptions. She also requested straightforward navigation so visitors aren't discouraged by complex or prolonged steps in learning about different NGOs.

**Inspiration sites:**
- [TED](https://www.ted.com/) - Deniz liked how different TED talks and their categories could be replaced with different social matters and the NGOs that promote them; she also liked how the website's colors are on-theme and repetitive, helping certain topics stand out in a minimalistic yet attractive way
- [DoSomething.org](https://dosomething.org/) - Deniz found it attractive as inspiration for a site designed to appeal to young individuals and students. She liked some of the bright colors but doesn't want it to be too overwhelming. She liked the call-to-action sections mostly because they were easy and encouraging for gaining involvement.
